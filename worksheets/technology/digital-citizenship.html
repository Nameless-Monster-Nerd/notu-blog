Being a Good Digital Neighbor — Online Safety & Kindness

📚 Grade 6-8 💻 Technology ⭐ UDL Designed 🏫 District 75
NYS Technology Standards + ISTE
DL2: Digital Citizenship — Respectful and responsible online behavior

🌟 Big Idea: The Internet Is a Neighborhood

Just like your physical neighborhood, the internet has:

- **Streets** (websites and apps)

- **Houses** (your accounts and profiles)

- **Neighbors** (other users)

- **Rules** (terms of service and laws)

**The Golden Rule applies online too:** Treat others how you want to be treated.

🛡️ **Safety Rules:**
1. Never share your full name, address, school, or phone number online
2. If something feels wrong — tell a trusted adult
3. Use strong passwords (a sentence you can remember works great!)
4. Think before you post — once it's online, it's hard to take back

>

💬 **Digital Kindness:**
- Would you say it to their face? Then don't type it.
- If someone is mean to you online — don't respond. Block them. Tell an adult.
- Stand up for others. Send a kind message. Be the good neighbor.

**Remember:** Behind every screen is a real person with real feelings.

✏️ Interactive Activities (2)

1

✅ Safe or Not?

Scenario: 'A stranger asks for your address in a game chat.' Safe or not? 'Your friend sends you a funny meme.' Safe or not? 'A website says you won a prize but needs your phone number.' Safe or not?

2

💬 Rewrite It

Mean message: 'You're so bad at this game, go away!' Rewrite it as something kind and helpful.

👩‍🏫 Teacher Guide — UDL Adaptations

Engagement: Let students choose their preferred activity. Use the interactive boxes for typed responses, or print and complete by hand. Offer individual or pair work.

Representation: Read all text aloud. Use the 🛡️ icon as a visual cue. Color-code and simplify instructions as needed.

Action/Expression: Students can type in the interactive boxes, print and write, draw on paper, or verbally answer. Use the contenteditable boxes for typed responses.

Roleplay scenarios with puppets or pictures. For sensory learners: use red/green cards (red=unsafe, green=safe). For non-verbal: create a digital safety picture book.

🧠
Sensory Connection: 🟢 Hold a green card for safe choices, 🔴 red for unsafe. Use a stop sign visual when a scenario is unsafe.
