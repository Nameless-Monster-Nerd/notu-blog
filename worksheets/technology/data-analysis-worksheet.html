🧠 Big Idea: Collecting, organizing, and reading data using tally charts and bar graphs 📊 Visual Guide: Example bar graph showing favorite fruits with labeled axes: x-axis = fruit names, y-axis = number of students
📋 Follow These Steps:
🔍
Step 1 What Is Data?

Data is information. Your height is data. Your favorite color is data. How many pets you have? Data! Data helps us understand the world.

📋
Step 2 Tally Your Class

Survey 5 people: 'What's your favorite color?' Make a tally mark for each answer. Count the tallies.

📊
Step 3 Draw a Bar Graph

Use your tally data. Draw a bar graph: each color gets a bar. How high? As high as the tally count!

✏️
Step 4 Read the Chart

Look at the weather chart: Monday 72°, Tuesday 75°, Wednesday 68°, Thursday 70°, Friday 80°. Which day was hottest? Coldest?

🔗
Step 5 Match It

Teacher Guide — UDL Adaptations
Use real classroom data: lunch choices, shoe colors, birthdays. For sensory: build 3D bar graphs with blocks. For non-verbal: point to the tallest bar.
🧠 Sensory: 📦 Stack blocks to make a 3D bar graph. The tallest stack = the most. 👀 Sort colored objects and count.
