CS1: Computer Science Basics — Algorithms are step-by-step instructions

🌟 Big Idea: Computers Follow Steps — Just Like You Do

An **algorithm** is a set of step-by-step instructions to complete a task. Sounds fancy, right? But you already use algorithms every day!

**Your morning routine is an algorithm:**

1. Wake up

2. Brush teeth

3. Get dressed

4. Eat breakfast

5. Pack bag

If you skip a step or change the order — things get messy! Same with computers. **Computers are very fast, but they only do exactly what you tell them.**

💡 **Try this:** Write the algorithm for making a peanut butter sandwich. Give it to someone and have them follow it EXACTLY. If your instructions said "put peanut butter on bread" but didn't say which side... you might get a surprise!

>

🖥️ **Computer algorithms are the same.** A video game is just millions of tiny instructions running super fast! "If player presses A, jump. If enemy touches player, lose life."

✏️ Interactive Activities (2)

1

📝 Algorithm Challenge

Write step-by-step instructions to: (a) Make a bed (b) Cross a street safely (c) Find a book in the library. Trade with a friend and test each other's algorithms!

2

🐞 Debug It!

Find the mistake in this sandwich algorithm: 1. Open bread bag 2. Put bread on plate 3. Close bread bag 4. Eat the sandwich (Wait... where's the peanut butter?)

👩‍🏫 Teacher Guide — UDL Adaptations

Engagement: Let students choose their preferred activity. Use the interactive boxes for typed responses, or print and complete by hand. Offer individual or pair work.

Representation: Read all text aloud. Use the 🧩 icon as a visual cue. Color-code and simplify instructions as needed.

Action/Expression: Students can type in the interactive boxes, print and write, draw on paper, or verbally answer. Use the contenteditable boxes for typed responses.

Use physical activities: give students 3-step algorithm cards to arrange. For non-verbal students: use picture-sequence cards. For sensory: use physical objects (blocks, toys) to act out algorithms.
