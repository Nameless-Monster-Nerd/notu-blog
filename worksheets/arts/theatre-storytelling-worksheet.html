🧠 Big Idea: Using voice, body, and imagination to tell stories 📊 Visual Guide: Story Mountain: Character 🧑 → Setting 🏠 → Problem ❓ → Solution ✅ → The End 🎬
📋 Follow These Steps:
🔍
Step 1 You Can Be Anyone

Theatre is imagining together. An actor pretends to be someone else. The audience agrees to believe them. Magic!

🎭
Step 2 Character Walk

Walk across the room as: (1) Happy grandparent — slow, warm (2) Nervous student — fast, fidgety (3) Proud superhero — tall, confident.

📖
Step 3 Story in 3 Objects

Pick 3 objects from the room (a pen, a cup, a book). Tell a 1-minute story using all 3. Perform it!

✏️
Step 4 Build Your Story

Use the Story Mountain to plan your own 1-minute story: Character → Setting → Problem → Solution.

Teacher Guide — UDL Adaptations
Use puppets for shy students. Create a 'story box' with textured props. For non-verbal: use picture cards to build a story sequence.
🧠 Sensory: 🎭 Try different voices — feel where each vibrates. 🎪 Move through different imaginary spaces (small box, big field).
