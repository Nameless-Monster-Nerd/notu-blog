Who Eats Who? The Secret Food Web in Your Backyard

Education Matters Writer 📅 June 30, 2026 ⏱️ 4 min 📂 Science

Every living thing is connected. One small change can affect the whole neighborhood!

### Did You Know You Share Your Neighborhood with a Food Web? A "food web" isn't something you can see — but it's happening all around you, every second of every day. It's a map of **who eats who** in nature. Every living thing plays a role:
### 🌻 Producers — The Power Plants of Nature Plants use sunlight to make their own food (photosynthesis). They start EVERY food chain. Without producers, nothing else can survive! **Examples:** Grass, flowers, trees, algae ### 🐇 Consumers — The Eaters Consumers eat other living things: - **Herbivores** eat plants (rabbits, deer, caterpillars, squirrels) - **Carnivores** eat meat (foxes, eagles, snakes, hawks) - **Omnivores** eat both (bears, raccoons, humans — you!) ### 🍄 Decomposers — Nature's Cleanup Crew Mushrooms, worms, and bacteria break down dead things and return nutrients to the soil. They're nature's recyclers!
🌍 **Food Chain Example:** Sun → 🌻 Grass → 🐛 Cricket → 🐸 Frog → 🐍 Snake → 🦅 Hawk **What happens if the crickets disappear?** The frogs have less food → fewer frogs → snakes have less food → fewer snakes → hawks have less food. **Everything is connected!**
🧪 Hands-On Learning Activity
## 🕸️ Learning Activity: Build Your Own Food Web Using the animals below, draw arrows showing who eats who. The arrow always points FROM the food TO the eater. ``` ☀️ Sun → 🌻 Grass 🌻 Grass → 🐇 Rabbit 🌻 Grass → 🐛 Cricket 🐛 Cricket → 🐸 Frog 🐸 Frog → 🐍 Snake 🐇 Rabbit → 🦊 Fox 🐍 Snake → 🦅 Hawk 🦊 Fox → 🦅 Hawk ``` **Challenge Question:** If ALL the frogs disappeared from this web, which animals would be affected?
