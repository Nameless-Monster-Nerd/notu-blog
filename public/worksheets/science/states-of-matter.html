🔍 Let's Learn

Everything around us is made of matter. Matter comes in 3 states:

🧊
Solid
Stays the same shape
💧
Liquid
Takes the shape of its container
💨
Gas
Fills the whole space

👩‍🏫 Teacher Guide — UDL Adaptations:
Engagement: Let students choose to cut & glue, draw, or point to answers. Use real objects (ice cube, water cup, balloon) for hands-on exploration.
Representation: Read instructions aloud. Use the visual icons (🧊💧💨) as non-verbal cues. Color-code the columns for easy identification.
Action/Expression: Students can point, circle, cut/paste, or verbalize answers. For non-verbal students, use a 3-card choice system (solid/liquid/gas cards).
Sensory Note: Let students hold an ice cube (solid), feel water (liquid), and feel air from a fan (gas) before starting.
Extension: Ask "What happens when ice melts?" (solid → liquid) and "What happens when water boils?" (liquid → gas).
