Waves — How Sound and Light Travel

📚 Grade 6-8 🔬 Science ⭐ UDL Designed 🏫 District 75
NGSS — Next Generation Science Standards
PS4.A: Wave Properties — Waves carry energy from place to place

🌟 Big Idea: Waves Carry Energy

Drop a pebble in water. See the ripples? Those are **waves** — energy moving from one place to another.

**Sound waves** travel through air (or water, or solids). When someone talks, their vocal cords vibrate, push air molecules, and those vibrations travel to your ear.

🔇 **In space, no one can hear you scream.** Sound needs a medium (air, water, solid) to travel through. Space is empty — no air means no sound!

**Light waves** are different. They can travel through empty space. That's why we can see the sun from 93 million miles away!

🌈 **Wavelength and frequency:**
- Long wavelength = low frequency = low pitch sound (boom!)
- Short wavelength = high frequency = high pitch sound (tweet!)

>

**Visible light** is just a tiny part of the electromagnetic spectrum. Radio waves, microwaves, X-rays — they're all waves!

**Real-world connection:** Your phone uses radio waves (invisible!) to connect to the internet. Your microwave uses waves to heat food. Your eyes detect light waves so you can read this!

✏️ Interactive Activities (2)

1

🌊 Ripple Demo

Fill a bowl with water. Drop one pebble. Watch the waves. Now drop two pebbles at once. What happens when the wave circles meet?

2

🎵 High vs Low

Hum a LOW note (like a tuba). Feel your chest vibrate. Now hum a HIGH note (like a whistle). Feel your head vibrate. Where does each frequency feel different?
