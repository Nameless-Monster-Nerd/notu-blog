Patterns in Art & Music — We Are All Rhythm

📚 Grade 6-7 🎨 Arts ⭐ UDL Designed 🏫 District 75
NYS Learning Standards for the Arts
MU1: Music — Express ideas through sound and rhythm

🌟 Big Idea: Patterns Make Things Beautiful

A **pattern** is something that repeats. In music, it's a rhythm. In art, it's a design. In nature, it's everywhere!

**In Music:**

- A drum beat: BOOM... tap... BOOM... tap...

- A melody: Do-Re-Mi-Fa-So-La-Ti-Do

- A song verse: Verse 1 → Chorus → Verse 2 → Chorus

**In Art:**

- Stripes on a zebra

- Petals on a flower

- Tiles on a floor

- Dots in a polka dot dress

🎵 **Pro tip:** Your heartbeat is a pattern! So is your breathing. Walking is a pattern (left-right-left-right). **You are made of patterns!**

>

🖌️ **Famous artist Yayoi Kusama** uses polka dots in ALL her work. She said dots are like the pattern of stars in the universe.

**Patterns help our brains predict what's coming next. That's why they feel safe and satisfying!**

✏️ Interactive Activities (2)

1

🥁 Body Rhythm

Clap a pattern: CLAP... tap-tap... CLAP... tap-tap. Now stomp it. Now snap it. Can you invent your own 4-beat pattern?

2

🎨 Pattern Tile

Draw a simple shape (triangle, circle, star). Repeat it across the page in a row. Now change the color every other shape. You just made a pattern!

👩‍🏫 Teacher Guide — UDL Adaptations

Engagement: Let students choose their preferred activity. Use the interactive boxes for typed responses, or print and complete by hand. Offer individual or pair work.

Representation: Read all text aloud. Use the 🥁 icon as a visual cue. Color-code and simplify instructions as needed.

Action/Expression: Students can type in the interactive boxes, print and write, draw on paper, or verbally answer. Use the contenteditable boxes for typed responses.

Use body percussion (clap, stomp, snap) for students who struggle with instruments. For sensory: provide textured pattern cards (bumpy, smooth, fuzzy). For visual: show alternating color strips.

🧠
Sensory Connection: 🥁 Tap different surfaces (wood, metal, fabric) in a pattern. Feel the vibrations. 👋 Clap a rhythm together. Let the student lead!
