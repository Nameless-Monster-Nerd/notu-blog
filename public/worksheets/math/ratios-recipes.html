RP: Ratios and Proportional Relationships — Understand ratios and proportions

🌟 Big Idea: A Ratio Compares Two Things

A **ratio** tells us how much of one thing compared to another.

Think about making **lemonade**:

- 1 cup lemon juice + 3 cups water

- Ratio: 1:3 (one to three)

- If you double it: 2:6 (still tastes the same!)

🍕 **Pizza ratio:**
A perfect cheese pizza has:
1 part sauce : 2 parts cheese : 1 part crust
That's a ratio of 1:2:1

>

🥤 **Orange juice:**
Most concentrated juice says "mix 1 part concentrate with 3 parts water"
That's ratio 1:3
If you use too much water (1:5)... watery!
Too little (1:1)... too sweet!

**Ratios keep things balanced.** That's why your favorite recipes taste the same every time — the ratios don't change, even when you make more or less!

✏️ Interactive Activities (2)

1

🥤 Juice Challenge

You have 1 cup of concentrate. The ratio is 1:4 (concentrate:water). How much water do you add? Now you have 2 cups of concentrate — how much water?

2

🍪 Cookie Math

A cookie recipe needs 2 cups flour for every 1 cup sugar. You want to make a half batch. How much flour? How much sugar?
