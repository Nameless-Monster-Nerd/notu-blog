Models & Simulations — Testing Ideas Without Breaking Things

📚 Grade 6-8 💻 Technology ⭐ UDL Designed 🏫 District 75
NYS Technology Standards + ISTE
IT2: Modeling and Simulation — Use models to understand systems

🌟 Big Idea: Test It Virtually First

Before building a $1 billion airplane, engineers build a **model** — a smaller or virtual version. They test it in a **simulation** — a computer program that acts like the real world.

✈️ **Real examples:**
- Pilots train in flight simulators before flying real planes
- Weather scientists use computer models to predict hurricanes
- Video game designers model physics (gravity, collision) to make games feel real
- Architects build 3D models of buildings before construction

**Why simulate?** It's cheaper, faster, and SAFER. If a virtual plane crashes, nobody gets hurt. You just fix the design and try again!

🎮 **Did you know?** The physics in Minecraft (gravity, water flow, redstone circuits) is a simulation! You're testing ideas every time you build.

**Your turn:** A city wants to know where to build a new playground. What data would you need? A map? Population numbers? Where kids live? That's a model!

✏️ Interactive Activities (2)

1

🏗️ Paper Airplane Simulator

Fold 3 different paper airplane designs. Test each and record: how far it flies, how straight, how many loops. Which design is best?

2

🌧️ Weather Predictor

Look at clouds outside. Draw what you see. Predict: will it rain in the next 2 hours? Check later. How accurate was your model?
