🧠 Big Idea: Clearly defining a problem before designing a solution 📊 Visual Guide: Problem Definition Template: 4 boxes — WHO (user), WHAT (need), LIMITS (constraints), SUCCESS (criteria)
📋 Follow These Steps:
🔍
Step 1 Define First!

Before solving a problem, you must DEFINE it clearly. Vague: 'My room is messy.' Clear: 'A 12-year-old needs a storage system for clothes, books, and toys that costs under $30.'

❌
Step 2 Vague to Clear

Rewrite these vague problems clearly: (1) 'The bus is too crowded.' (2) 'Lunch break is too short.' (3) 'My backpack is too heavy.'

📋
Step 3 Write a Design Brief

Fill in the Design Brief for: 'Design a better school desk.' Who is the user? What do they need? What are the limits? How will you know it works?

✂️
Step 4 Sort It

Cut out the 6 statements. Glue each under: PROBLEM, CONSTRAINT, or SUCCESS CRITERIA.

✏️
Step 5 Draw It

Draw a picture of the problem 'Backpack is too heavy.' Label what hurts and what would help.
