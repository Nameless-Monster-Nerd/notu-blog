Building Bridges — What Makes a Structure Strong?

📚 Grade 6-7 ⚙️ Engineering ⭐ UDL Designed 🏫 District 75
NGSS Engineering Design + NYS Technology
ETS1.B: Developing Solutions — Generate and test multiple solutions

🌟 Big Idea: Shape = Strength

Have you noticed that bridges have **triangles** in them? That's not an accident. Triangles are the strongest shape in engineering!

**Why triangles?** When you push on a triangle, the forces spread evenly across all three sides. A square or rectangle will collapse — the sides push out and the corners bend.

🏗️ **Three Types of Bridges:**

>

1. **Beam Bridge** — A flat board across two supports. Simple, but can only span short distances.
2. **Arch Bridge** — A curved shape that pushes force down and outward. Very strong!
3. **Suspension Bridge** — Cables hang from tall towers. Can span MILES (like the Golden Gate Bridge!).

>

🔍 **Engineers ask three questions:**
- Will it hold the weight? (Strength)
- Will it last? (Durability)
- Can we build it? (Feasibility)

**Your challenge:** A bridge must hold a toy car. What shape will you build?

✏️ Interactive Activities (2)

1

🏗️ Build & Test

Use paper, tape, and books. Build 3 bridges: flat paper, folded paper (accordion), paper rolled into tubes. Test each with pennies. Which holds the most?

2

🔺 Shape Test

Make a square out of straws or popsicle sticks. Push gently on a corner. Now make a triangle. Push. Which is stronger?

Engagement: Let students choose their preferred activity. Use the interactive boxes for typed responses, or print and complete by hand. Offer individual or pair work.

Representation: Read all text aloud. Use the 🌉 icon as a visual cue. Color-code and simplify instructions as needed.

Action/Expression: Students can type in the interactive boxes, print and write, draw on paper, or verbally answer. Use the contenteditable boxes for typed responses.

🧠
