# 🌊 Waves, Sound & Light

**Grade:** 6-8 · **Standard:** NGSS PS4.A · **Read time:** 5 min

## 🌟 Big Idea

Waves carry energy without carrying matter. Sound and light are both waves — but they behave very differently. Understanding waves means understanding how we hear music, see colors, and communicate across the world.

> 💡 **Did you know?** Sound waves travel about 4 times faster in water than in air. That's why whales can communicate across entire oceans!

## 🧪 Core Concepts

### What Is a Wave?

A wave is a disturbance that transfers energy from one place to another. The material it travels through is called the **medium** — sound needs air, water, or solids, but light doesn't need any medium at all.

### Wave Properties

| Property | What It Measures | Example |
|----------|-----------------|---------|
| **Amplitude** | Height of the wave (energy) | Louder sound = bigger amplitude |
| **Frequency** | How many waves per second (Hz) | Higher pitch = higher frequency |
| **Wavelength** | Distance between wave peaks | Red light has longer wavelength than blue |
| **Speed** | How fast the wave travels | Light: 300,000 km/s — Sound: 343 m/s |

### Sound vs. Light Waves

| Feature | Sound Waves | Light Waves |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **Type** | Mechanical (needs medium) | Electromagnetic (no medium needed) |
| **Speed** | 343 m/s in air | 300,000,000 m/s |
| **Frequency range** | 20 Hz – 20,000 Hz (human hearing) | 400–700 trillion Hz (visible spectrum) |
| **Can travel through space?** | ❌ No | ✅ Yes |

### 🎵 Lab Connection: The Studio as a Wave Lab

Every day at Mohikontok Sound Lab, we work with sound waves. Microphones convert sound wave energy (vibrations in the air) into electrical signals. Speakers do the reverse — turning electricity back into sound waves. The frequency of a note determines its pitch: A440 (440 Hz) is the standard tuning note. Amplitude determines volume — measured in decibels (dB). Our soundproof room controls how waves bounce, absorb, and travel, giving us clean recordings.

## 🎥 Audio-Visual Learning

**Try this at home:** Fill a bowl with water. Drop a pebble in and watch the ripples — those are waves! Now tap the side of the bowl gently, then harder. The harder tap creates bigger ripples (higher amplitude). That's exactly how sound works — more energy = bigger waves = louder sound. In the studio, we visualize sound waves on a computer screen using a tool called a waveform display. You can see the amplitude (loudness) and frequency (pitch) of any recording.
