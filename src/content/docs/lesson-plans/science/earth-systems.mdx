# 🌍 Earth Systems

**Grade:** 6-8 · **Standard:** NGSS ESS2.A · **Read time:** 5 min

## 🌟 Big Idea

Earth is a system of interconnected parts — the geosphere (rock and land), hydrosphere (water), atmosphere (air), and biosphere (life). Changes in one sphere affect all the others. Understanding these connections helps us predict weather, protect ecosystems, and even design better buildings.

> 💡 **Did you know?** The Amazon rainforest creates its own weather! Trees release so much water vapor that it forms clouds and rain — the forest makes its own atmosphere.

## 🧪 Core Concepts

### Earth's Four Spheres

| Sphere | What It Includes | Example |
|--------|-----------------|---------|
| **Geosphere** | Rocks, mountains, soil, Earth's crust and core | The Bronx's bedrock foundation |
| **Hydrosphere** | All water — oceans, rivers, lakes, groundwater | The East River and Long Island Sound |
| **Atmosphere** | The layer of gases surrounding Earth | The air we breathe and sound travels through |
| **Biosphere** | All living things — plants, animals, humans | Bronx's parks, wildlife, and people |

### How Spheres Interact

When it rains (atmosphere), water flows into the ground (hydrosphere → geosphere), plants grow (biosphere), and the air cools (atmosphere). Every action ripples through the system.

### 🎵 Lab Connection: Sound and the Atmosphere

Sound waves travel through the atmosphere — and the atmosphere changes how sound behaves. Hot air makes sound travel faster; cold air slows it down. Humidity affects how far sound carries. That's why outdoor concerts sound different on a humid summer night vs. a crisp winter afternoon. ## 🎥 Audio-Visual Learning

**Try this at home:** Go outside on a hot day and a cold day. Clap your hands and listen to how the sound carries. On a hot day, sound waves travel faster through the less-dense warm air — the clap sounds sharper and travels farther. On a cold day, the denser air slows sound down. That's the atmosphere (one of Earth's systems) affecting how you experience sound every day!
