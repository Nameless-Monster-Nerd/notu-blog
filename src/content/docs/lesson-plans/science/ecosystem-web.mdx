# 🌿 Ecosystems & Food Webs

**Grade:** 6-8 · **Standard:** NGSS LS2.A · **Read time:** 5 min

## 🌟 Big Idea

Every living thing is connected. Energy flows through ecosystems like sound waves through a room — from the sun (the source) all the way to the top predators. When one link in the chain breaks, the whole system feels it.

> 💡 **Did you know?** A single oak tree can support over 500 species of insects, birds, and mammals — it's like a whole apartment building for the forest!

## 🧪 Core Concepts

### What is an Ecosystem?

An **ecosystem** is a community of living organisms (plants, animals, microbes) interacting with their non-living environment (air, water, soil, sunlight). Think of it like a recording studio: every piece of gear, every cable, every acoustic panel plays a role in making the final sound work.

### Food Chains vs. Food Webs

| Concept | Definition | Studio Analogy |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| **Producer** | Makes its own food (plants, algae) | The power source — like the electricity that runs the mixer |
| **Consumer** | Eats other organisms | The engineer — using the signal from the mic |
| **Decomposer** | Breaks down dead matter | The acoustic foam — absorbing and recycling energy |
| **Food Chain** | Single path of energy flow | One signal path: mic → cable → interface → speaker |
| **Food Web** | Multiple interconnected paths | The full patch bay — every signal can go multiple ways |

### Energy Pyramid

Only about **10%** of energy transfers from one trophic level to the next. The rest is lost as heat — just like how sound energy dissipates in a room.

```
    🦅 Top Predator (1 kcal)
    🐍 Secondary Consumer (10 kcal)
    🐇 Primary Consumer (100 kcal)
    🌿 Producer (1,000 kcal)
```

### 🎵 Lab Connection: The Studio Ecosystem

At **Mohikontok Sound Lab**, we think of the studio as its own ecosystem. The microphone (producer) captures sound. The cables and interface (primary consumers) carry the signal. The DAW and effects (secondary consumers) process it. And the speakers (decomposers) release it back into the room. If any link is missing — a bad cable, a dead mic, a blown speaker — the whole system suffers. Just like in nature, every part matters.

## 🎥 Audio-Visual Learning

**Activity: The Sound of an Ecosystem**

Go outside and sit quietly for 5 minutes. Close your eyes. Listen to the soundscape around you:

1. **Birds** (top predators or consumers) — high, sharp sounds
2. **Insects** (primary consumers) — buzzing, clicking
3. **Wind through plants** (producers) — rustling leaves
4. **Your own breathing** — you're part of the ecosystem too!

**At the lab:** We record these soundscapes and layer them like a food web — each sound occupies its own frequency range, just like each organism occupies its own niche. Try recording 30 seconds of your backyard and see how many "species" of sound you can identify.
