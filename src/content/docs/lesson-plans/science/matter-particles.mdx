# 🔬 Matter & Particles

**Grade:** 6-8 · **Standard:** NGSS PS1.A · **Read time:** 5 min

## 🌟 Big Idea

Everything around you — your desk, the air you breathe, your own body — is made of **matter**. And matter is made of tiny particles called **atoms** and **molecules**.

> 💡 **Did you know?** A single drop of water contains about 1.5 sextillion (that's 1,500,000,000,000,000,000,000) molecules!

## 🧪 Core Concepts

### Atoms: The Building Blocks

Atoms are the smallest unit of a chemical element. There are 118 known types of atoms (elements), and they combine in endless ways to make everything in the universe.

| Element | Symbol | Where You Find It |
|---------|--------|-------------------|
| Hydrogen | H | Water (H₂O), stars |
| Oxygen | O | Air we breathe, water |
| Carbon | C | All living things |
| Iron | Fe | Your blood, Earth's core |

### States of Matter

Matter exists in four main states:

| State | Particle Arrangement | Shape | Volume | Example |
|-------|---------------------|-------|--------|---------|
| **Solid** | Tightly packed, vibrating | Fixed | Fixed | Ice |
| **Liquid** | Close but flowing | Container-shaped | Fixed | Water |
| **Gas** | Far apart, fast-moving | Fills container | Variable | Steam |
| **Plasma** | Ionized particles | Fills space | Variable | Lightning |

### 🔬 Lab Connection: Sound & Matter

Sound travels differently through each state of matter. In solids, particles are packed tight — sound waves move fast. In gases, particles are spread out — sound moves slower. That's why you can hear a train coming by putting your ear to the rail!
