# ⚡ Energy in Motion

**Grade:** 6-8 · **Standard:** NGSS PS3.A · **Read time:** 5 min

## 🌟 Big Idea

Energy is everywhere — it's what makes things move, change, and happen. It can't be created or destroyed, only transformed from one form to another.

> 💡 **Did you know?** The energy from the sun that reaches Earth in one hour could power the entire world for a year!

## 🧪 Core Concepts

### Kinetic Energy — Energy of Motion

Anything that moves has kinetic energy. The faster it moves and the heavier it is, the more kinetic energy it has.

> **Formula:** KE = ½mv² (mass × velocity squared)

### Potential Energy — Stored Energy

Energy waiting to be released. Like a stretched rubber band, a ball at the top of a hill, or a charged battery.

| Energy Type | Example | Description |
|-------------|---------|-------------|
| **Kinetic** | A rolling ball | Moving energy |
| **Gravitational Potential** | A book on a high shelf | Height stores energy |
| **Elastic Potential** | A stretched spring | Shape stores energy |
| **Chemical Potential** | Food, batteries | Bonds store energy |

### Conservation of Energy

Energy never disappears — it just changes form. When you drop a ball, gravitational potential energy becomes kinetic energy. When it hits the ground, kinetic energy becomes sound and heat.

### 🎵 Lab Connection: Sound Energy

Sound is **mechanical energy** traveling through matter. In the studio, we capture sound energy (vibrations in the air) and convert it to electrical energy (microphone signal), then back to sound (speakers). That's energy transformation in action!

## 🎥 Audio-Visual Learning

**Try this at home:** Drop a ball from different heights and listen. Higher drop = more kinetic energy = louder sound. That's energy transformation you can hear! In the studio, we use this principle every day — microphones are just energy transformers.
