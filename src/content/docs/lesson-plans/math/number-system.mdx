# 🔢 Number System

**Grade:** 6-8 · **Standard:** CCSS 6.NS · **Read time:** 5 min

## 🌟 Big Idea

Numbers are more than just counting — they describe position, direction, value, and magnitude. The number system includes positive and negative numbers, fractions, decimals, and absolute value. Understanding the number line means understanding how everything relates.

> 💡 **Did you know?** The concept of zero as a number was invented independently in India, Mesopotamia, and Mesoamerica. Before zero, people had no way to represent "nothing" mathematically!

## 🧪 Core Concepts

### Integers — Positive and Negative

Integers are whole numbers that can be positive (above zero), negative (below zero), or zero itself. Think of temperature: 5°F above freezing is +5, 5°F below is -5.

### Rational Numbers

Any number that can be written as a fraction (a/b where b ≠ 0). This includes integers, fractions, and decimals that repeat or terminate.

### Absolute Value

The distance a number is from zero on the number line — always positive. | -7 | = 7 because -7 is 7 steps from zero.

| Concept | Definition | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Integer** | Whole number, positive or negative | -3, 0, 15 |
| **Rational number** | Can be written as a fraction | ½, -2.5, 0.333... |
| **Absolute value** | Distance from zero | \| -8 \| = 8 |
| **Opposites** | Same distance from zero, opposite sides | 5 and -5 |

### 🎵 Lab Connection: Audio as Numbers

Every sound in a recording is represented as numbers. When you record audio, the microphone captures the sound wave and converts it to digital values — positive and negative numbers that represent the wave's amplitude at each moment in time. A quiet sound might be values near zero; a loud sound reaches high positive and negative numbers. The absolute value tells us the loudness regardless of direction. That's why digital audio is measured in "bits" — more bits mean more numbers to represent the sound more accurately!

## 🎥 Audio-Visual Learning

**Try this at home:** Open any audio recording app on your phone. Record yourself saying "hello" softly, then loudly. Watch the waveform — the quiet "hello" makes small waves near zero (small absolute value), the loud one makes big waves far from zero (large absolute value). The center line is zero — positive numbers go up, negative numbers go down. You're literally watching the number system in action!
