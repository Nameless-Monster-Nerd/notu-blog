# 📐 Ratios & Recipes

**Grade:** 6-7 · **Standard:** Common Core RP.A · **Read time:** 5 min

## 🌟 Big Idea

A **ratio** is a comparison between two quantities. When you're cooking, mixing audio, or building anything — ratios are the secret formula.

> 💡 **Did you know?** The perfect cup of chai has a ratio of 1:1:1 — equal parts milk, water, and tea leaves. Bengalis have known this for centuries!

## 🧪 Core Concepts

### What is a Ratio?

A ratio compares two numbers. It can be written three ways:
- **With a colon:** 3:1
- **As a fraction:** ¾
- **In words:** "3 to 1"

### Equivalent Ratios

Just like equivalent fractions, ratios can be scaled up or down:

| Recipe | Flour (cups) | Sugar (cups) | Ratio |
|--------|-------------|--------------|-------|
| Small batch | 2 | 1 | 2:1 |
| Medium batch | 4 | 2 | 2:1 |
| Large batch | 6 | 3 | 2:1 |

### Proportions

A proportion says two ratios are equal: 2:1 = 4:2. Cross-multiply to check: 2 × 2 = 1 × 4 ✓

### 🎵 Lab Connection: Audio Ratios

In the studio, we use ratios every day:
- **Mix ratios** — 3 parts vocal to 2 parts instrumental
- **Compression ratio** — 4:1 means every 4dB above threshold becomes 1dB
- **Signal-to-noise ratio** — higher is better, like 80dB signal to 20dB noise = 4:1

## 🎯 Learning Activity

### Recipe Scaler

A cookie recipe calls for 3 cups flour : 1 cup sugar : ½ cup butter. Scale it:

| Batch Size | Flour | Sugar | Butter |
|------------|-------|-------|--------|
| Original | 3 cups | 1 cup | ½ cup |
| Double (×2) | | | |
| Triple (×3) | | | |
| Half (×½) | | | |

## 🎥 Audio-Visual Learning

**Try this:** Make a simple ratio-based drink (lemonade: 1 part lemon juice, 2 parts sugar, 8 parts water). Taste it. Then change the ratio — what happens? That's the same principle audio engineers use when mixing a song!
