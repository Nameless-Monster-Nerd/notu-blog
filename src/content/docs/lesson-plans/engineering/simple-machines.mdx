# ⚙️ Simple Machines

**Grade:** 6-8 · **Standard:** NGSS ETS1.A · **Read time:** 5 min

## 🌟 Big Idea

Every complex machine — from a car engine to a recording studio console — is built from **six simple machines**. Master these, and you understand how everything works.

> 💡 **Did you know?** The human body is a system of levers. Your forearm is a lever, your elbow is the fulcrum, and your muscles provide the force!

## 🧪 Core Concepts

### The Six Simple Machines

| Machine | What It Does | Real-World Example | Mechanical Advantage |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Lever** | Multiplies force | See-saw, crowbar | Fulcrum position |
| **Pulley** | Changes direction of force | Flagpole, curtain | Number of wheels |
| **Inclined Plane** | Reduces force over distance | Ramp, slide | Length ÷ height |
| **Wedge** | Splits things apart | Knife, axe | Thinner = better |
| **Screw** | Converts rotation to linear | Jar lid, drill bit | Thread spacing |
| **Wheel & Axle** | Multiplies rotation | Door knob, steering wheel | Radius ratio |

### Mechanical Advantage

Simple machines make work easier by trading **force for distance**. A longer ramp needs less force but covers more ground. A shorter ramp needs more force but covers less ground.

### 🎵 Lab Connection: Studio Machines

The recording studio is full of simple machines:
- **Levers** — Faders on a mixing console are levers!
- **Screws** — Every mic stand, every rack screw
- **Wheel & Axle** — Reel-to-reel tape machines, turntables
- **Pulleys** — Soundproof curtain tracks, lighting rigs

## 🎥 Audio-Visual Learning

**Try this:** Use a spoon as a lever to open a paint can lid. Feel how the fulcrum (the rim of the can) multiplies your force. Now try without using the rim — it's much harder! That's the power of simple machines.
