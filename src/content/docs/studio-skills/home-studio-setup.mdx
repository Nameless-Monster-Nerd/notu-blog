# 🏠 Home Studio Setup — What You Actually Need

**Level:** 🟢 Beginner | **Read Time:** 8 min

> *"I've seen $10,000 studios that sound worse than $500 setups. It's not about the gear — it's about knowing what matters."* — Mr. Nas

---

## The Philosophy

Most "home studio starter guides" are written by people trying to sell you gear. This one isn't.

**The truth:** You can make professional-quality recordings with:
- A decent interface
- One good mic
- A treated room (even basic treatment)
- Your ears

Everything else is optional.

## 🎚️ The Interface

**This is your most important purchase.** Don't cheap out.

**What to look for:**
- ✅ 2 inputs minimum (for stereo or dual-mic recording)
- ✅ Phantom power (+48V) for condenser mics
- ✅ Direct monitoring (hear yourself with zero latency)
- ✅ USB-C or USB 3.0
- ❌ Built-in effects (you don't need them)

**Our pick:** Focusrite Scarlett 2i2 (3rd gen). It just works.

---

## 🎤 The Mic

**Start with a dynamic mic.** Condensers pick up too much room noise — and if your room isn't treated, a condenser will sound worse.

**SM58:** The AK-47 of microphones. Indestructible, sounds good on everything, and cheap. Every studio should have one.

**When to upgrade:**
- Get a large-diaphragm condenser (AT2020, $100) when your room is treated
- Get a ribbon mic (AEA R84, $1,000+) when you know exactly why you need it

---

## 🎧 Headphones vs Monitors

**Start with headphones.** Good monitors need a treated room. Headphones work anywhere.

**ATH-M50x:** The standard. Flat response, comfortable for long sessions, foldable.

**When to add monitors:**
- When you've treated your room
- When you need to feel the bass (headphones can't do this)
- Budget: $300+ for a pair (KRK Rokit, Yamaha HS5)

---

## 🪑 The Desk Setup

You don't need a "studio desk." A sturdy table works. But pay attention to:

1. **Monitor height** — ears at tweeter level
2. **Speaker placement** — equilateral triangle with your head
3. **Cable management** — keep power cables away from audio cables
4. **Comfort** — you'll spend hours here. Get a good chair.

---

## 📊 What to Skip (For Now)

| Item | Why Skip | Cost Saved |
|------|----------|------------|
| Expensive XLR cables | $20 cables sound the same as $100 cables | $80 |
| Acoustic foam | DIY panels work better for less | $100 |
| Outboard gear | Plugins sound just as good | $500+ |
| "Studio" monitors | Headphones + treated room > cheap monitors | $300 |
| Subwoofer | Causes more problems than it solves | $400+ |
