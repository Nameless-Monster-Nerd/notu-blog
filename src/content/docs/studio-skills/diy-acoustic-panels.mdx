# 🧱 DIY Acoustic Panels — Build, Mount, Tune

**Level:** 🟡 Intermediate | **Read Time:** 12 min

> *"A $30 DIY panel outperforms a $100 foam panel every single time. Science, not marketing."* — Mr. Nas

---

## Why Build Your Own?

Store-bought acoustic foam is **overpriced and underperforms**. A DIY rigid fiberglass panel absorbs down to 125 Hz. Foam panels barely touch 500 Hz. For the price of one "pro" foam panel, you can build 3-4 DIY panels that actually work.

---

## 🛒 Materials (Per Panel)

| Item | Cost | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Rigid fiberglass (OC 703, 2" thick, 2'x4') | $18 | HVAC supply or Amazon |
| Fabric (burlap, muslin, or Guilford of Maine) | $5 | 1 yard per panel |
| Spray adhesive (3M Super 77) | $4 | 1 can covers 4 panels |
| 1x3 pine lumber (8ft) | $3 | For the frame |
| Wood screws + corner brackets | $2 | |
| Picture hanging wire + D-rings | $2 | For wall mounting |
| **Total per panel** | **~$34** | |

---

## 🔨 Step-by-Step Build

### 1. Build the Frame
Cut 1x3 lumber into:
- 2 pieces at 47" (top/bottom)
- 2 pieces at 22.5" (sides)

Assemble with wood screws + corner brackets. The frame should be 2" deep to fit the fiberglass.

> 📸 *[Photo: Frame assembled at the Lab]*

### 2. Cut the Fiberglass
Score with a utility knife + straightedge. Snap over the edge of a table. Wear gloves + mask — fiberglass dust is nasty.

### 3. Wrap in Fabric
Lay fabric face-down. Place frame on top. Insert fiberglass. Wrap fabric around the back and staple. Pull tight — wrinkles kill the look.

> 🎥 *[Video: Fabric wrapping technique — how to get museum-quality corners]*

### 4. Mount on Wall
Attach D-rings to the back of the frame. Install screws + wall anchors. Hang like a picture frame.

**Critical:** Leave a **2" air gap** behind the panel. Use spacers (1x2 blocks) between the panel and the wall. This doubles low-frequency absorption.

---

## 📍 Placement Strategy

| Position | Panels Needed | Effect |
|----------|--------------|--------|
| First reflection points (side walls) | 2-4 | Cleans up stereo image |
| Behind listening position | 1-2 | Stops rear wall bounce |
| Ceiling cloud (above mix position) | 1-2 | Kills floor-ceiling flutter |
| Corners (bass traps) | 2-4 | Tames low-end buildup |

**First reflection points** = where you'd see the speaker in a mirror placed on the wall while sitting at your mix position.
