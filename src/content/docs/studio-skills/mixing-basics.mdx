# 🎛️ Mixing for Beginners — EQ, Compression, Reverb

**Level:** 🟢 Beginner | **Read Time:** 12 min

> *"Mixing isn't magic. It's three tools used well: EQ to make space, compression to control dynamics, reverb to create depth."* — Mr. Nas

---

## 🎯 The Big Picture

A good mix sounds like **one performance in one room** — even if the parts were recorded separately. Your job as a mixer is to make the listener forget they're listening to a recording.

Three tools do 90% of the work:
1. **EQ** — carve out space for each instrument
2. **Compression** — smooth out the dynamics
3. **Reverb** — place everything in a believable space

---

## 🎚️ 1. EQ (Equalization)

### What It Does
EQ lets you boost or cut specific frequency ranges. Think of it as **organizing a dinner party** — every instrument needs its own seat at the table.

### The Frequency Map

| Range | What Lives Here | What to Cut | What to Boost |
|-------|----------------|-------------|---------------|
| 20-60 Hz | Sub bass, kick drum thump | Rumble, HVAC noise | Kick punch |
| 60-250 Hz | Bass, kick body, low vocals | Mud (200-300 Hz) | Warmth (100 Hz) |
| 250-500 Hz | Low-mid body | Boxiness (400 Hz) | — |
| 500 Hz-2 kHz | Presence, honk | Nasality (1 kHz) | Clarity (2 kHz) |
| 2-5 kHz | Attack, snap | Harshness (3 kHz) | Definition (4 kHz) |
| 5-10 kHz | Air, sparkle | Sibilance (7 kHz) | Openness (8 kHz) |
| 10-20 kHz | High-end shimmer | Noise | Air |

### The Golden Rule
**Cut before you boost.** If something sounds muddy, cut the mud — don't boost the clarity. Cuts sound natural. Boosts sound processed.

### Practical Exercise
1. Put an EQ on your vocal track
2. Sweep a narrow boost (high Q) from 200 Hz to 5 kHz
3. Find the frequencies that sound BAD
4. Cut those by 3-6 dB
5. Now your vocal sits in the mix without fighting anything

## 📦 2. Compression

### What It Does
Compression makes loud parts quieter and quiet parts louder. It **reduces the dynamic range** so the performance sits consistently in the mix.

### The Controls (Plain English)

| Control | What It Does | Start With |
|---------|--------------|------------|
| **Threshold** | How loud before compression kicks in | -18 dB |
| **Ratio** | How much compression (2:1 = gentle, 10:1 = hard) | 3:1 |
| **Attack** | How fast it kicks in (fast = catches transients) | 10 ms |
| **Release** | How fast it lets go (fast = pumping, slow = smooth) | 50 ms |
| **Make-up Gain** | Turn the overall level back up | Match bypass level |

### When to Use It
- **Vocals:** 3:1 ratio, medium attack, medium release
- **Drums:** 4:1 ratio, fast attack, fast release
- **Bass:** 4:1 ratio, slow attack, medium release
- **Master bus:** 2:1 ratio, slow attack, auto release

### The "Gain Reduction" Rule
Aim for **3-6 dB of gain reduction** on most sources. More than 6 dB sounds squashed. Less than 3 dB isn't doing much.

---

## 🌊 3. Reverb

### What It Does
Reverb creates the illusion of space — a room, a hall, a chamber. It tells the listener's ear **how big the space is** and **how far away the sound is**.

### Types of Reverb

| Type | Sound | Best For |
|------|-------|----------|
| Room | Small, natural | Drums, vocals (intimate) |
| Hall | Big, lush | Strings, pads, ballads |
| Plate | Smooth, bright | Vocals, snare |
| Spring | Bouncy, vintage | Guitar, dub |
| Chamber | Warm, complex | Everything |

### The "Dry/Wet" Rule
- **Lead vocals:** 15-25% wet
- **Snare:** 20-30% wet
- **Guitar:** 25-40% wet
- **Background vocals:** 30-50% wet
- **Master bus:** 5-10% wet (glue reverb)

### Pro Tip: Pre-Delay
Add 20-40ms of pre-delay to your reverb. This lets the dry signal hit first (clear and present), then the reverb blooms after (space and depth). The listener hears both clarity AND space.

---

## 🎯 The 3-Step Mix Workflow

### Step 1: Balance (Levels)
Set all faders so nothing clips. Get a rough balance. The vocal should be clear but not overpowering.

### Step 2: Space (EQ + Pan)
- Pan instruments left/right (drums center, guitars wide, keys in between)
- EQ each track to sit in its own frequency zone
- Check in mono — if something disappears, your panning needs work

### Step 3: Depth (Compression + Reverb)
- Compress to control dynamics
- Add reverb to place instruments in a believable space
- Lead vocal = closest (least reverb), drums = medium, pads = farthest

## 📊 Quick Reference

| Instrument | EQ Move | Compression | Reverb |
|------------|---------|-------------|--------|
| Kick | Cut 300 Hz, boost 60 Hz | 4:1, fast | None or room |
| Snare | Cut 400 Hz, boost 200 Hz | 4:1, fast | Plate, 25% |
| Bass | Cut 200 Hz, boost 100 Hz | 4:1, slow | None |
| Guitar | Cut 300 Hz, boost 3 kHz | 3:1, medium | Room, 30% |
| Vocals | Cut 300 Hz, boost 4 kHz | 3:1, medium | Plate, 20% |
