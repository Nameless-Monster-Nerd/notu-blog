# 🔇 Soundproofing a Room for Under $200

**Level:** 🟢 Beginner | **Read Time:** 8 min

> *"You don't need a million-dollar build to get a usable recording space. You need to know where the sound leaks."* — Mr. Nas

---

## The Philosophy

Soundproofing isn't about spending money. It's about **finding the gaps and closing them**. A $20,000 build can fail if you miss one gap. A $200 build can work if you seal everything.

At Mohikontok Sound Lab, we learned this the hard way — right next to Bruckner Blvd with trucks rumbling past 24/7. Here's what actually works on a budget.

## 🔧 Step 1: Seal the Door (Most Important!)

The door is the biggest leak in any room. A typical door has a **1/4" gap** at the bottom — that's like leaving a window open for sound.

**What to do:**
1. **Weatherstripping** — apply foam tape around the door frame (jamb side, not the stop)
2. **Drop seal / door sweep** — install on the bottom. When the door closes, the sweep drops to seal the gap
3. **Caulk the frame** — run a bead of caulk where the frame meets the wall

**Cost:** ~$27
**Effect:** Blocks ~5-8 dB of flanking noise

> 💡 **Pro tip:** Use a dollar bill to test your seal. Close the door on the bill — if it slides out easily, your seal isn't tight enough.

---

## 🪟 Step 2: Window Plug

Windows are thin glass — terrible for sound isolation. A **window plug** is a removable insert that blocks sound.

**How to build:**
1. Cut 2" rigid foam board to fit snugly inside the window frame
2. Wrap in heavy fabric (moving blanket or felt)
3. Add a handle (duct tape loop works)
4. Press into the window frame — friction holds it

**Cost:** ~$30
**Effect:** Blocks ~10 dB of window transmission

## 🧱 Step 3: DIY Acoustic Panels (Absorption)

These don't "soundproof" (stop sound from leaving), but they **clean up the sound inside** the room — less echo, clearer recordings.

**Quick build:**
1. Buy rigid fiberglass (OC 703 or equivalent) — 2" thick
2. Cut to 2'x4' panels
3. Wrap in breathable fabric (muslin or burlap)
4. Mount 2" away from the wall (air gap improves bass absorption)

**Cost:** ~$60 for 2 panels
**Effect:** Reduces reverb time by 30-50%

> 📸 *[Photo: DIY panel build process at the Lab]*

---

## 🧥 Step 4: Moving Blanket Fort

This is the **cheapest trick** that works immediately:

1. Hang moving blankets on walls using command hooks or a clothesline
2. Layer them — 2 layers = better than 1
3. Create a "fort" around your recording position

**Cost:** ~$30
**Effect:** Instant reduction in room echo. Not pretty, but it works.

> 🎥 *[Video: Before/after recording test with moving blankets]*

---

## 🧪 Step 5: The Dollar Store Caulk Trick

Walk around your room with a flashlight at night. Have someone stand outside. Everywhere you see light = everywhere sound leaks.

**Seal everything:**
- Baseboards → caulk
- Outlets → foam outlet gaskets ($2 each)
- Light switches → same
- Pipe penetrations → caulk + putty
- HVAC vents → magnetic vent covers

**Cost:** ~$15
**Effect:** The biggest bang for your buck
