# 🇧🇩 Bengali Heritage

From the rivers of Bengal to the streets of the Bronx — our language, our poetry, our stories travel with us.

## Language & Poetry

Bengali (Bangla) is spoken by over 230 million people worldwide. It's the language of Rabindranath Tagore — the first non-European to win the Nobel Prize in Literature — and Kazi Nazrul Islam, the rebel poet whose words still ignite movements.

> *"Where the mind is without fear and the head is held high..."*
> — Tagore, *Gitanjali*

At Mohikontok Sound Lab, we write kobita (poetry) in Bangla because some feelings only rhyme in the mother tongue.

## Festivals & Traditions

- **Pohela Boishakh** — Bengali New Year, celebrated with music, food, and new beginnings
- **Ekushey February** — International Mother Language Day, born from the Bengali Language Movement
- **Durga Puja** — The biggest Bengali festival — art, music, dance, community

## Bengali in the Bronx

The Bronx is home to a growing Bengali community. From Parkchester to Norwood, you'll hear Bangla spoken in bodegas, at community centers, and — of course — at Mohikontok Sound Lab.
